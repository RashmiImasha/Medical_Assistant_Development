## **Apple Inc.** 

## Form 10-K Summary — Fiscal Year Ended September 28, 2024 

_NASDAQ: AAPL  |  Cupertino, California_ 

## Company Overview 

Apple Inc. designs, manufactures, and markets smartphones, personal computers, tablets, wearables, and accessories, and sells a range of related services. The company's fiscal year is the 52- or 53-week period ending on the last Saturday of September; fiscal 2024 spanned 52 weeks. 

Core product lines include iPhone (iOS), Mac (macOS), iPad (iPadOS), and Wearables, Home and Accessories (Apple Watch, AirPods, Apple Vision Pro, Apple TV, HomePod). Services span Advertising, AppleCare, Cloud Services, Digital Content (App Store, Apple Music, Apple TV+, Apple Arcade, Apple News+, Apple Fitness+), and Payment Services (Apple Card, Apple Pay). 

The company sells directly through retail and online stores and a direct sales force, and indirectly through carriers, wholesalers, retailers, and resellers. In fiscal 2024, 38% of net sales came through direct channels and 62% through indirect channels. 

As of October 18, 2024, Apple had 15,115,823,000 shares outstanding and 23,301 shareholders of record. The aggregate market value of shares held by non-affiliates was approximately $2.63 trillion as of March 29, 2024.

## Principal Risk Factors 

Apple's 10-K (Item 1A) discloses a broad set of risks; the most material themes include: 

- Global and macroeconomic conditions — inflation, interest rates, currency fluctuations, and geopolitical tensions can materially affect results. 

- Intense competition — markets are characterized by aggressive pricing, short product cycles, and rapid technological change; competitors often compete on price and imitate Apple's products and IP. 

- Customer concentration — reliance on third-party cellular carriers (38% of trade receivables) and manufacturing vendor relationships (two vendors representing 44% and 23% of non-trade receivables). 

- Supply chain concentration — many components are single- or limited-sourced, and hardware manufacturing is concentrated with outsourcing partners primarily in China mainland, India, Japan, South Korea, Taiwan, and Vietnam. 

- Foreign exchange and interest rate risk — as a large net receiver of non-U.S. dollar currencies, a strengthening dollar pressures net sales and margins; a hypothetical 100 bps rate increase would reduce investment portfolio fair value by roughly $2.8 billion. 

- Legal and regulatory exposure — ongoing litigation and regulatory scrutiny globally; the EU State Aid Decision alone resulted in a $10.2 billion net one-time tax charge and a $15.8 billion payment obligation to Ireland in FY2024. 

- Tax law and rate changes — uncertain tax positions ($22.0 billion gross unrecognized tax benefits as of FY2024) and evolving international tax frameworks. 

- Cybersecurity and data privacy — as a global technology and services provider processing significant customer data. 

## Growth Drivers 

- Services momentum — Services net sales grew 13% to $96.2 billion (24.6% of total net sales) with gross margin of 73.9%, well above Products' 37.2%, driven by advertising, App Store, and cloud services; this remains Apple's primary margin-accretive growth lever. 

- Product innovation and ecosystem expansion — new launches in FY2024 included iPhone 16 lineup, Apple Watch Series 10, AirPods 4, updated Macs and iPads, and Apple Intelligence, a new generative-AI personal intelligence system integrated across iOS 18, macOS Sequoia, iPadOS 18, watchOS 11, and visionOS 2. 

- Geographic diversification — Europe (+7%) and Rest of Asia Pacific (+4%) outpaced the overall 2% company growth rate, partly offsetting an 8% decline in Greater China. 

- Capital return program — a new $110 billion share repurchase authorization (announced May 2024) and a raised quarterly dividend (to $0.25/share) continue to return substantial capital to shareholders while shrinking share count (down ~500 million shares in FY2024). 

- Cost discipline in Products — Products gross margin improved to 37.2% from 36.5% on cost savings, despite unfavorable currency and mix effects. 

- Strong balance sheet liquidity — $140.8 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities supports continued R&D investment ($31.4 billion in FY2024, +5%), acquisitions, and capital returns.
